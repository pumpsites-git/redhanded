# Florida Public Records Requests — Criminal Case Data

## Template Email

Subject: Public Records Request — Criminal Case Data with Judge Assignments (Chapter 119, F.S.)

---

Dear [County] Clerk of Court,

Pursuant to Florida's Public Records Law, Chapter 119, Florida Statutes, I am requesting the following public court records in electronic format (CSV or Excel preferred):

**Requested Data:**
All criminal case records (felony, misdemeanor, and criminal traffic) filed or disposed in 2024-2025, including the following fields for each case:

1. Case number
2. Assigned/presiding judge name
3. Case type (felony, misdemeanor, criminal traffic)
4. Primary charge(s) / statute
5. Disposition (adjudicated guilty, withheld adjudication, dismissed, etc.)
6. Sentence type (prison, jail, probation, community control, etc.)
7. Sentence length/duration
8. Filing date
9. Disposition date
10. Defendant demographics (race, gender, age) if publicly available

This request specifically excludes any sealed, expunged, or confidential records. I am only requesting information that is already part of the public court record.

Per §119.07(1)(a), F.S., the custodian shall provide copies of public records in the medium requested if the records are maintained in that medium. I am requesting electronic delivery to minimize staff time and cost.

If there are any fees associated with this request, please provide an estimate before processing. Per §119.07(4), F.S., fees are limited to the actual cost of duplication.

Thank you for your assistance with this request.

Sincerely,
Bryan Deering
bryan@yourwarpaint.com

## Notes
- Florida law requires response within a "reasonable time" — typically 5-10 business days
- If they refuse, cite §119.07 and request written explanation
- Many clerks already have this data in exportable format from their case management systems
- Some may charge per-page fees for electronic records — typically minimal for bulk data exports
- Broward already has a commercial API — may offer bulk export as alternative

## Expected Data Volume
- Miami-Dade: ~50,000+ criminal cases/year
- Broward: ~40,000+ criminal cases/year
- Palm Beach: ~25,000+ criminal cases/year
- Total across all counties: ~200,000+ cases/year
